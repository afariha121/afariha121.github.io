MPA Candidate · Public Policy

Hello, I’m Fariha.

I am a Master of Public Affairs candidate with a concentration in Public Policy at the Truman School of Government and Public Affairs, University of Missouri–Columbia. My work is rooted in data-driven policy analysis, monitoring government initiatives, advocacy, and nonprofit program management — always in service of equity and social impact for vulnerable populations.

My journey began at the University of Dhaka, where I studied Development Studies and graduated ranked first in the Faculty of Social Sciences. From coordinating water projects in the coastal belts of Bangladesh to managing program evaluation data in Missouri, I have learned that good policy begins with listening to the people it is meant to serve.

Research Interests

Data-driven policy & program evaluation

At the Institute of Public Policy, I manage evaluation data for the Missouri Teen Pregnancy Prevention (MOTPP) project — cleaning, coding, and merging pre- and post-program surveys from roughly 500 participants across the TOP SRAE and State PREP programs, so that evidence, not guesswork, shapes what happens next…

Read More

Governance, institutional trust & reform in South Asia

From analyzing Bangladesh’s Reform Commission reports at DAIRA to studying how the Rohingya crisis is addressed in multilateral forums at SIPG, I ask how institutions earn — and lose — the trust of their citizens. My mixed-methods work on institutional trust in South Asia was presented at the 2026 MPSA Conference in Chicago…

Read More

Nonprofit management & humanitarian response

With Give Bangladesh Foundation, I led a climate-adaptive water sustainability project that brought drinkable water to nearly 30,000 people, and managed 60 volunteers supporting marginalized communities in northern Bangladesh. I care about how nonprofits build capacity, raise funds, and deliver when it matters most…
